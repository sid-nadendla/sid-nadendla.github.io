Cyber-Physical-Human systems comprises mainly of three fundamental entities, namely

  • Cyber: Infrastructure that provides/supports communication, storage and other information technology (IT) services.
  • Physical: Physical devices such as sensors and actuators.
  • Human: People (residents, workers and/or users) within the system.

Most of the current services are designed based on only two of the above three entities. For instance, social networks, recommender systems and crowdsourcing are examples of cyber-human systems where human agents interact with cyber infrastructure. On the other hand, disaster management and human-machine interfaces such as flight-cockpits are examples for physical-human systems. At the same time, Internet-of-Things (IoT), smart grids and industrial control systems are some of the practical examples of cyber-physical systems. However, smart living applications rely on the framework of cyber-physical-human systems since it provides a common platform to support interaction between technology (cyber-physical infrastructure) and people.
